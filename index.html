Swami Vivekananda

The man whose ideals are a great source of inspiration for the Indian youth

Swami Vivekananda, 2nd from the right, at the World Parliament of Religions, Chicago, 1893


Here's a time line of Swami Vivekananda's life:

  • 1863 - on 12th January Narendranath Datta (his original name, shortened to Narendra or Naren) was born in Calcutta(captial of British India on that time, Now it is Kolkata), India
  • 1871 - at the age of eight, Narendranath enrolled at Ishwar Chandra Vidyasagar's Metropolitan Institution, where he went to school
  • 1879 - he was the only student to receive first-division marks in the Presidency College entrance examination
  • 1880 - Narendra joined Keshab Chandra Sen's Nava Vidhan, which was established by Sen after meeting Ramakrishna and reconverting from Christianity to Hinduism
  • 1881 - Narendra first met Ramakrishna Paramahamsa, who became his spiritual focus
  • 1883 - Narendra became a member of a Freemasonry lodge and of the Sadharan Brahmo Samaj in his twenties, a breakaway faction of the Brahmo Samaj led by Keshab Chandra Sen and Debendranath Tagore
  • 1884 - completed a Bachelor of Arts degree and Narendra's father's sudden death left the family bankrupt; creditors began demanding the repayment of loans, and relatives threatened to evict the family from their ancestral home. Narendra, once a son of a well-to-do family, became one of the poorest students in his college
  • 1886 - after the death of Ramakrishna, when his devotees stopped funding, he and other disciples of Ramakrishna decided to make a dilapidated house at Baranagar their new math ( known as Baranagar Math or Ramakrishna Math). The house crumbled to dust in 1897. In Antpur, in the Christmas Eve of 1886, Narendra and eight other disciples took formal monastic vows. Narendranath took the name "Swami Vivekananda"
  • 1891 - Narendra left the monastery as a "Parivrâjaka"— the Hindu religious life of a wandering monk, "without fixed abode, without ties, independent and strangers wherever they go"
  • 1893 - Narendra left Bombay for Chicago on 31st May with the name "Vivekananda" and visited several cities in Japan (including Nagasaki, Kobe, Yokohama, Osaka, Kyoto and Tokyo), China and Canada en route to the United States, reaching Chicago on 30th July. The Parliament of the World's Religions opened on 11th September at the Art Institute of Chicago, as part of the World's Columbian Exposition. "Sisters and brothers of America!" At these words, Vivekananda received a two-minute standing ovation from the crowd of seven thousand and gave a brief speech representing India and Hinduism
  • 1895 - Vivekananda founded the periodical "Brahmavadin"
  • 1896 - he met Margaret Elizabeth Noble an Irish woman who would become "Sister Nivedita"
  • 1897 - In Colombo, he gave his first public speech in the East. From there on, his journey to Calcutta was triumphant. Vivekananda travelled from Colombo to Pamban, Rameswaram, Ramnad, Madurai, Kumbakonam and Madras, delivering lectures; later these lectures, published as "Lectures from Colombo to Almora". "Arise, awake, and stop not till the goal is reached", the famous quote of Vivekananda was said in January 26 in kumbakonam, tamilnadu. On 1st May Vivekananda founded the "Ramakrishna Mission"
  • 1898 - Vivekananda composed "Khandana Bhava–Bandhana", a prayer song dedicated to Ramakrishna
  • 1899 - Despite declining health, Vivekananda left for the West for a second time and during this visit, Vivekananda established "Vedanta Societies" in San Francisco and New York and founded a "shanti ashrama" (peace retreat) in California
  • 1900 - he went to Paris for the Congress of Religions, then visited Brittany, Vienna, Istanbul, Athens and Egypt
  • 1902 - On 4th July, Vivekananda awoke early, went to the monastery at Belur Math and meditated for three hours. He taught Shukla-Yajur-Veda, Sanskrit grammar and the philosophy of yoga to pupils, later discussing with colleagues a planned Vedic college in the Ramakrishna Math. At 7:00 pm Vivekananda went to his room, asking not to be disturbed; he died at 9:20 p.m. while meditating at the age of 39. According to his disciples, Vivekananda attained "Mahasamādhi"; the rupture of a blood vessel in his brain was reported as a possible cause of death
